For Scouts, potential Scouts, and Parents/Guardians

We are pleased that you are interested in our Scout Troop, and in Scouting in general. Scouting is a tremendous youth development program that; teaches valuable skills, develops new and varied interests, builds leadership in individuals, and creates friendships and memories that will last a lifetime. Scouts Honor!

Troop 214 was chartered in 1967, and is just over 53 years young. Our membership average over the past several years has been between 30-35 Scouts. We have a very active, year-round Scout program.

We meet on Monday nights each week in our own "Scout Hut" at Benson Memorial United Methodist Church (BMUMC) in north Raleigh. We'll take this opportunity to thank our chartering organization online, as they are a great partner and have provided great support to Troop 214 as one of their long-standing youth ministries.

We also have a girls troop that was chartered in February 2019. Its membership average is currently 15 Scouts. The girls troop camps monthly and meets on the same nights as the boys troop.

We camp every month, and attend week-long Scout summer camp every summer.

Our campouts teach outdoor skills and appreciation of natural areas, but also provide advancement opportunities and adventure. Some examples of our campouts include; backpacking, caving, climbing/rappelling, whitewater rafting, canoeing, kayaking, sailing, scuba diving, and anything and everything else the Scouts decide to do in their annual planning meetings.

The troops are youth-led, with adults acting in advisory, safety, and transportation roles. The adults also have as much fun as the youth. We encourage parents to consider a troop role in which they can best use their talents and skills to help (i.e., Troop Committee, Assistant Scoutmaster, Patrol Advisor, Merit Badge Counselor). It is not required, but working with the youth is very rewarding and the more people working with it means a stronger and better troop for the youth.

We also provide community service throughout the year, have troop elections twice annually, Courts of Honor (awards ceremony) every four months, and go on a variety of day-trips and patrol outings annually.

Over 165 Scouts have earned the coveted Eagle rank in Troop 214. We don't judge the quality of our troop by that number, but we're proud of every one of those individuals.

If you want to learn more, please contact the boys Scoutmaster or Senior Patrol Leader or the girls Scoutmaster. Better yet, come visit any of our weekly meetings. The weekly troop meetings start at 7:00pm and end at 8:30pm. We don't meet on a variety of national or school holidays, so you should check our online calendar to be sure. Also be sure to check out some of our online trip photos to see some of the campouts we go on. And if you are brand new to Scouting you should check out Be A Scout for a good intro on what the Scouting program is all about.

Thanks for visiting!
